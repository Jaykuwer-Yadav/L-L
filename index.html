Calculation Rules & Legal Framework
Everything you need to know about Leave and License agreement stamp duty in Maharashtra.
How is Stamp Duty calculated?
Under Article 36A of the Bombay Stamp Act, 1958, Stamp Duty is calculated at a flat rate of 0.25% of the "Total Consideration" (the Taxable Base). The Total Consideration includes the sum of all rent to be paid for the license period, any non-refundable deposits, and a 10% per annum notional interest on the refundable security deposit.
What is Notional Interest?
The state government assumes that if a landlord receives a refundable security deposit, they will earn interest on it. Therefore, a notional interest rate of 10% per annum is calculated on the refundable security deposit for the duration of the license period. This calculated interest is added to the total rent and non-refundable deposits to form the "Total Consideration".
What are the Registration Fees?
Registration charges are fixed by the government based on where the property is located:
  • Urban Areas: Property within Municipal Corporation boundaries carries a fixed registration fee of ₹1,000.
  • Rural Areas: Property outside Municipal Corporation boundaries carries a fixed registration fee of ₹500.
Is there a minimum Stamp Duty?
Yes. If the calculated stamp duty is less than ₹100, the law mandates a minimum stamp duty of ₹100 (provided the total consideration is greater than 0). If there is no deposit, no rent, and no consideration, the stamp duty is ₹0.
